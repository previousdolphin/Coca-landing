The Ancient Engine

What is the I Ching?

The I Ching, or Book of Changes, is a 3,000-year-old Chinese text and one of the oldest operational manuals for decision-making in human history. Historically, it was not used as a crystal ball to predict the future, but rather as a strategic matrix to understand the present.

It is built on a binary system of broken (Yin) and solid (Yang) lines. These lines stack into sets of three (trigrams) and combine into 64 unique Hexagrams representing specific states of tension, transition, or balance.

Emperors and tacticians generated hexagrams not to be told what to do, but to understand the architecture of the moment so they could act with precision.

The Modern Refinery

Diet Coke by Unsubscribe LLC

The Diet Coke Oracle System takes the foundational logic of the I Ching and weaponizes it for the modern mind. Drawing inspiration from the Church of Conceptual Art, it rejects the mystical, New Age associations that often cloud modern interpretations. Instead, it treats the I Ching as pure code.

Most human inquiries are clouded by emotional noise, outcome bias, and vague complaints. Diet Coke forces the user to confront their ideas clinically. It is less of an app and more of a hostile editor driven by a large language model.

01

Mode 1: The Refinery

The AI evaluates the user's input for "oracle-grade precision." If a user asks a vague or emotionally loaded question, the system rejects it. It demands the problem be reframed into a precise, structural logic-gate. The pushback itself is the primary utility.

02

Mode 2: The Casting

Once structural clarity is achieved, the user casts the lines by answering a series of strict binary questions regarding the tension of their situation (e.g., Assert vs. Submit, Expand vs. Contract). This generates the architectural hexagram.

03

Mode 3: Interpretation

The system bypasses vague mysticism, mapping the ancient Richard Wilhelm translation of the I Ching directly onto the clinical parameters established during the Refinery phase.

04

Mode 4: The Discussion

The user engages in a brief, clinical discussion with the oracle about the result. The focus remains purely on structural alignment and operational mechanics rather than seeking advice or therapy.
